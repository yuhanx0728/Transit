<p>The <a href="/wiki/Port_Authority_of_Allegheny_County" title="Port Authority of Allegheny County">Port Authority's</a> bus system covers <a href="/wiki/Allegheny_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Allegheny County, Pennsylvania">Allegheny County</a>, and its service extends into small portions of neighboring <a href="/wiki/Beaver_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Beaver County, Pennsylvania">Beaver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Butler_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Butler County, Pennsylvania">Butler</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Westmoreland_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Westmoreland County, Pennsylvania">Westmoreland</a> counties. These counties also have their own transit systems, including several routes that run into <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Pittsburgh" title="Downtown Pittsburgh">Downtown Pittsburgh</a>, where riders can make connections with Port Authority service.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Route_renumbering_and_realignments">Route renumbering and realignments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_bus_routes_in_Pittsburgh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Route renumbering and realignments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1964 to 2010, the Port Authority used an alphanumeric numbering system. With the renumbering and realignments outlined in the Transit Development Plan in 2009, the present numbering system was adopted in April 2010.
</p><p>Light rail and busways are designated by the following colors:
</p>
<ul><li><span style="background-color:#EC1B24;border:1px solid #000">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;</span> Red Line—former 42S South Hills Village via Beechview light rail route</li>
<li><span style="background-color:#0088CE;border:1px solid #000">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;</span> Blue Line—former 47S South Hills Village via Overbrook and 47L Library via Overbrook light rail routes</li>
<li><span style="background-color:#AD7A4C;border:1px solid #000">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;</span> Brown Line—former 52 Allentown light rail route (eliminated in 2011)</li>
<li><span style="background-color:#00ad4c;border:1px solid #000">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;</span> Green—bus routes using the <a href="/wiki/West_Busway" title="West Busway">West Busway</a>, designated with a 'G'</li>
<li><span style="background-color:#9e0876;border:1px solid #000">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;</span> Purple—bus routes using the <a href="/wiki/Martin_Luther_King_Jr._East_Busway" title="Martin Luther King Jr. East Busway">Martin Luther King Jr. East Busway</a>, designated with a 'P'</li>
<li><span style="background-color:#F4811F;border:1px solid #000">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;</span> Orange—bus routes using the <a href="/wiki/I-279" class="mw-redirect" title="I-279">I-279</a> HOV lane, designated with an 'O'</li>
<li><span style="background-color:#ffc734;border:1px solid #000">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;</span> Yellow—bus routes using the <a href="/wiki/South_Busway" title="South Busway">South Busway</a>, designated with a 'Y'</li></ul>
<p>As a result of the Transit Development Plan and a series of service cuts, many routes have been reworked or eliminated.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus_operations">Bus operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_bus_routes_in_Pittsburgh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bus operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Route numbers operating seven days a week including holidays (4 am to 2 am daily) are listed in <span style="color:blue;"><b>Blue</b></span>. On most of the daily bus routes, they operate on a 60-minute interval after 7 pm Monday through Saturday and all day on Sunday and Holidays.</li>
<li>Route numbers operating Monday through Saturday with limited or no holiday hours (5 am to 10 pm weekdays; 7 am to 7 pm Saturdays) are listed in <span style="color:red;"><b>Red</b></span></li>
<li>Route numbers operating Monday through Friday only (5 am to 7 pm) are listed in <b>Bold</b>.</li>
<li>All other plain (non-bold) routes operate Monday through Friday during peak periods only (approx: 6 am to 9 am and 3 pm to 6 pm).</li></ul>
